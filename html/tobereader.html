To The Reader

This is not a regular book with a single subject. It is not even a conglomeration of different essays cleverly brought together by an intelligent editor. It may be more akin to an inscrutable word formed mysteriously by the letters accidentally falling from a word-building set kept on a table! Its only merit is that it is in Sanskrit and printed in Devanagari script!

These writings were spread over a long period of 55 years (1960-2015). Most of them have been printed also, sometimes with translations.

The idea of bringing them together is more out of fancy than the illusion that is may be useful someday to somebody!

The essays -short and long -as also the various books, have been brought together into a single volume with some sort of orderliness to aviod its being labelled `disarrayed'.

The only hope is that lovers of Sanskrit like you who may happen to glance through it, may exclaim, `not bad!'

As for myself, it is a standing monument of Divine Grace as pictured in the Mukrndamala Mukam Karoti Vacalam]

-S. H.
